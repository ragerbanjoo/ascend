## 2. What ASCEND is

ASCEND is a one-day Eucharistic Revival gathering — part of the broader National Eucharistic Revival movement — at Meydenbauer Center in Bellevue. The day includes talks (Chris Stefanick, Dr. Andrew & Sarah Swafford, Dr. Tim Gray), extended Eucharistic Adoration with a Confession window, sacred music (Marie Miller, Floriani), and Holy Mass celebrated by Archbishop Paul Etienne.

The retreat then continues Sunday with a Traditional Latin Mass at North American Martyrs Parish (Edmonds), prayer at St. James Cathedral, and a fellowship afternoon in Seattle.

## 11. Likely parent questions (and the short answers)

**Why so early Saturday?** We arrive at Meydenbauer at 6:30 AM so we can be in Eucharistic Adoration and Confession from 7:00 AM before the program opens at 9:00.

**My child isn't Catholic — can they still come?** Yes. ASCEND is open to everyone. During Mass, non-Catholics are invited forward for a blessing.

**What if my child has dietary restrictions?** Tell Gaby ahead of time. All meal stops (Sizzle & Crunch, Chick-fil-A, Kona Kitchen, anywhere they choose near Pike Place) have allergy-friendly options.

**What about Snoqualmie Pass?** We check WSDOT pass conditions the night before and adjust departure if needed. Mid-May is typically clear, but we always pad the schedule.

**Can my child bring their own car?** No. The group travels together in the three pre-vetted vehicles.

**Can my child be picked up early?** Only by a parent/guardian or an adult listed on the Section III "Authorized Early Pickup" line, coordinated with Gaby in advance by phone.

**What if there's a financial hardship?** Talk to Gaby — money is never the reason someone misses this trip.
